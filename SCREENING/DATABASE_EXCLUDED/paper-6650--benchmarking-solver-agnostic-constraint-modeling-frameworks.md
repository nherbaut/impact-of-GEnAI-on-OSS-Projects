  title: Benchmarking Solver-agnostic Constraint Modeling Frameworks
  authors: Roberto Penco, Pavle Žuljević, Damir Pintar, Marko Šoštarić
  abstract: 'Solver-Agnostic Constraint Modeling Frameworks (SaCMFs) aim to decouple constraint problem formulation from specific
    solving engines, thereby promoting portability, maintainability, and solver interchangeability. In this paper, we benchmark
    three prominent open-source SaCMFs, MiniZinc, Savile Row, and CPpy, across seven evaluation dimensions: modeling expressiveness,
    solver abstraction, code generation complexity, performance, usability, model fidelity, and community reliability. We
    introduce an extended version of the Automatic Constraint Model Generator (ACMG), powered by large language models (LLMs),
    to generate semantically equivalent models in each framework from natural language descriptions. This ensures an unbiased
    comparison of framework capabilities over a diverse set of CP/CSP benchmark problems. Our findings show that MiniZinc
    has the best combination of modeling capability, ease of use, and community support. Savile Row is the best at solver
    abstraction and performance, while CPpy is the best at integrating with Python, but it doesn’t have as much support from
    the rest of the ecosystem. The results make it apparent to researchers and practitioners which modeling frameworks to
    choose, and they suggest that ACMG is a good automated benchmarking tool for constraint modeling technologies.'
