  title: Preliminary Study of Automating Spot Control with Large Language Models (LLMs)
  authors: Roberto Reyes, Fang Tang
  abstract: Recent advances in Large Language Models (LLMs) have enabled significant progress in code generation, yet applying
    them to robotic control remains challenging due to hardware dependencies, low-level framework requirements, and the complexity
    of real-world environments. This work presents a preliminary study of a system that enables users to issue natural language
    commands to a robot and automatically generate executable ROS 2 control scripts using an open-source LLM. Specifically,
    our system integrates speech recognition, text-to-speech, and DeepSeek to translate user commands into executable ROS
    2 control scripts, bridging the gap between intuitive human instructions and low-level robotic operations. We validate
    the feasibility of our method on a real robot with proof-of-concept experiments, demonstrating that LLM-generated scripts
    can control the robot with minimum human intervention. The results highlight both the promise and limitations of current
    LLM-driven robotics and help identify areas for future improvement.
