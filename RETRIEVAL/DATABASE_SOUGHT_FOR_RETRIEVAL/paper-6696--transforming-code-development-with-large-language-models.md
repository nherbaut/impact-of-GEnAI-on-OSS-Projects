  title: Transforming Code Development with Large Language Models
  authors: Alex Kaplunovich
  abstract: In recent years, a plethora of code generation tools, products, and models have been released, varying in price,
    size, integration capabilities, and the resources required for their utilization. In our study, we explored a range of
    tools, from IDE-integrated “helpers” to open-source models, to identify their advantages, drawbacks, successes, and failures.
    Our research aims to assist developers and data scientists in navigating the evolving landscape of code assistants and
    preparing for potential challenges. We have explored several key methods and techniques, including LLM finetuning, code
    dataset selection, the use of quantized models to minimize GPU memory requirements, and the implementation of techniques
    like LoRA, QLoRA, and PEFT for efficient tuning. Additionally, we explored llama.cpp for running advanced models on moderate
    CPU instances. Our research compared existing AI coding methods, commercial models and open-source LLMs, evaluating their
    performance, benefits, and limitations. We have also identified the missing tools and possible future trends. Our findings
    are intended to guide the software community through the rapidly advancing realm of Generative AI for coding, helping
    people to select solutions that best fit their specific needs, budget, and technological experience.
